Portrait subject, Bollywouldn't

Summer / Autumn 2022

I co-developed the concept for the imaginary film Kali Ka Choti Behen, co-writing an excerpt of the film script. This was based on my experiences within queer and feminist activist communities. The concept was turned into a portrait created by visual artist TextaQueen as part of their solo exhibition Bollywouldn't, held at the A4 Centre for Contemporary Asian Art in Sydney, Australia. In the portrait, I'm the head on the left with one of my arms holding up a dustpan and brush.

Cycling officer, Chit Chat Chai

Autumn 2022

Cycling is cheap, fun, quick for short journeys and has a low environmental impact, but is still very inaccessible to most of the UK population. To help encourage active travel for Manchester's South Asian communities, I organised and delivered women-only cycle training for learners at Chit Chat Chai, a project offering free basic women-only English in South Manchester and to local residents. This activity was funded by Big Bike Revival

Organiser, Over Here Zine Fest 2022

Summer 2022

I organised the second Over Here Zine Fest, a zine fair showcasing the work of global majority creatives and makers with a focus on artists based in the north of England. Over Here took place in Manchester at the People's History Museum as part of its Migration theme. I also created my own zines for Over Here (see image below) which I sold and exchanged at the event. Zines included 'Have You Seen Milhouse?' about the time my cat went missing, and 'Eulogy', a tribute to my grandmother who passed away this year.
